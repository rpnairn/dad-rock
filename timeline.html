Dad Rock: The Hits of the 90s

1991: Release of Nirvana's "Nevermind"


Nirvana's second album, "Nevermind," released in September 1991, became an instant hit, defining the grunge movement. Its lead single, "Smells Like Teen Spirit," became an anthem for Generation X and made Nirvana a household name. The album transformed alternative rock into a dominant force in the music industry.

1991: Pearl Jam's "Ten" Album Released


Pearl Jam's debut album "Ten," released in 1991, was a defining moment in the grunge era. The album's tracks like "Alive," "Even Flow," and "Jeremy" became staples of 90s rock radio. Pearl Jam’s raw, powerful sound and socially aware lyrics made them one of the most influential bands of the decade.

1993: Smashing Pumpkins' "Siamese Dream" Released


Smashing Pumpkins' 1993 album "Siamese Dream" was a groundbreaking release that blended alternative rock with heavy, layered guitar sounds. Tracks like "Today" and "Disarm" showcased Billy Corgan's unique vision and solidified the band as one of the key players in 90s rock music.

1994: Soundgarden's "Superunknown" Released


Soundgarden's "Superunknown," released in 1994, took the band’s heavy, riff-driven sound to new heights. With hits like "Black Hole Sun" and "Spoonman," the album became one of the most influential in the grunge movement, defining the sound of 90s alternative rock.

1997: Radiohead's "OK Computer" Released


In 1997, Radiohead released "OK Computer," an album that not only defined 90s alternative rock but also reshaped the direction of rock music. With its complex production, haunting lyrics, and themes of alienation, "OK Computer" is often regarded as one of the greatest albums of all time.

1994: Oasis' "Definitely Maybe" Released


Oasis' debut album "Definitely Maybe" arrived in 1994 and became the fastest-selling debut album in UK history. With its anthemic songs like "Live Forever" and "Supersonic," Oasis helped define the Britpop movement, bringing British rock back to the global stage.

1994: Green Day's "Dookie" Released


Green Day's third album, "Dookie," released in 1994, was a key moment in the pop-punk revolution of the 90s. Tracks like "Basket Case" and "When I Come Around" became massive hits, making Green Day one of the most influential bands of the decade and bringing punk rock to mainstream audiences.

1995: Alanis Morissette's "Jagged Little Pill" Released


Alanis Morissette's 1995 album "Jagged Little Pill" was a cultural phenomenon. Featuring the hit singles "You Oughta Know" and "Ironic," the album became an anthem for many 90s women and cemented Morissette as one of the decade's most influential artists.

1996: Beck's "Odelay" Released


Beck’s 1996 album "Odelay" was a groundbreaking release that blended a variety of genres, from folk to hip-hop to funk. With hits like "Where It's At" and "Devil's Haircut," Beck pushed the boundaries of alternative music and became one of the decade's most innovative artists.
